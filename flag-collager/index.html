Flag Collager

An organic flag mosaic grown by edge-color affinity, then rearranged by simulated annealing until neighboring borders agree.

Loading flags…
  1. Edge sampling Samples 20 pixels along each of the four edges of every flag, averaging into a single RGB color per side. The right edge of one flag is compared directly to the left edge of a potential neighbor.
  2. Pairwise distance matrix Builds a 265×265 matrix of Euclidean distances in RGB space — one for horizontal pairings (right-vs-left) and one for vertical (bottom-vs-top). These are the only numbers the layout algorithm sees.
  3. Organic growth Picks a seed flag and expands outward one flag at a time. Each candidate position scores every unplaced flag; whichever fits best (lowest average mismatch to existing neighbors) is placed there. Growth stops when the best available match exceeds a quality threshold — the algorithm refuses to place a flag that would visibly clash.
  4. Simulated annealing refinement Randomly proposes pair-swaps and accepts them probabilistically: good swaps always accepted, bad ones accepted with probability e ⊃ −ΔE/T where temperature cools on a schedule. After 30,000 steps the layout has settled toward a local minimum of total edge mismatch.
