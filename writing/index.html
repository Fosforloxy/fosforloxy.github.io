  • Essay · Philosophy · 2025

    Automating Empathy

    An essay arguing that AI cannot ethically replace human healthcare workers. Empathy is not a behavior pattern that can be simulated — it is an internal state grounded in biology — and a system incapable of genuine empathy is unfit for the core of clinical care. The case for premature AI integration in medicine, I argue, is driven by cost-cutting rather than concern for patients.

Finn Mulligan · New York
